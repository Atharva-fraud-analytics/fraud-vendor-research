Falcon Decision Engine

The central nervous system of the platform. It orchestrates the ingestion of transaction data via high-speed APIs, compiles behavioral profiles in real-time, calls upon machine learning models to generate a risk score, and executes user-defined rules to output a final decision (Approve, Decline, Review) within milliseconds.

  • Maintains stateful behavioral profiles (B-tags).
  • Sub-millisecond latency for payment processing.

AI, Machine Learning & Risk Scoring

Explore the methodology behind FICO's proprietary neural network models. This section explains how Falcon translates complex, multi-dimensional customer behavior into a simple, actionable 1-999 risk score, and visualizes how institutions set alert thresholds.

The 1-999 Scoring Methodology

Falcon assigns a score between 1 and 999 to every transaction. A higher score indicates a higher probability of fraud.

  • ✓
    Low Score (1-500): Typical behavior, high confidence of legitimacy. Automatically approved.
  • ⚠
    Medium Score (501-899): Slight deviations. May trigger soft rules, step-up authentication (OTP), or monitoring.
  • ✗
    High Score (900-999): Highly anomalous behavior. Typically triggers real-time decline or immediate analyst alert.
Behavioral Sorting Technology: Models use patented techniques to look at customer history, comparing the current transaction to past behaviors (velocity, geolocation, merchant type) using recursive variables called B-tags.

Score Distribution & Thresholds

Note: Most transactions cluster in the low-score range. Thresholds are optimized to balance Fraud Detection Rate (FDR) and False Positive Ratio (FPR).
